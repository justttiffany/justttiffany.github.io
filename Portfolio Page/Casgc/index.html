Arduino Computer Development and Programming Project

Overview

The California Space Grant Consortium/UCSD Summer Academy was sponsored by NASA to provide undergraduate engineering students at UCSD a chance to gain technical experience. I was placed on a team of all females because the goal was to promote more women in the STEM field and ensure that they stay within the engineering field.

Problem

Many teams in the science field can accomplish their goals and projects with the help of many resources in the world (i.e. Internet, science tool kits). However, sometimes these resources are not specific to their needs or are not readily available at the moment. This can slow done processes in development and delay workflow among teams.

Solution

Our team aimed on providing an Arduino Computer Development and Programming Science Kit for teams out there involved in Arduino development. We worked towards developing an Arduino program that collected data, such as temperature, pressure, humidity, and acceleration. This handy program was to provide an easy-to-use tool that users can just grab and use for their own projects.

Project Goal

The team's goal on this summer project was to develop a fully functional Arduino micro-controller that successfully gathered data regarding its surroundings. The end result was to be used for the launching of a near-space balloon by another team in the UCSD Summer Academy that summer.

What I did

As a development team member and intern in CaSGC, I worked alongside with one other team member to develop a program that collected temperature and pressure data of its surroundings.

Overview

I collaborated with one other team member to develop a program that detected the temperature and pressure of the surroundings and logged the data into the system. The program was to be merged with the other team members' tasks to develop a full functional Arduino micro-controller.

Skills

I utilized the Arduino IDE and Arduino programming language for this project. I also worked with the Arduino hardware, such as the micro-controller, and learned soldering.

Task

My teammate and I worked on implementing a program that detected the temperature and pressure its surroundings. The data was then collected and logged into the Arduino system and outputted to the users as results.

Experience

In retrospect, it was a great experience because it was my first summer project experience. I got to develop a program that would be used by many other students out there and I learned a new programming language!

Images

Below are some images of the Arduino micro-controller I worked with throughout the project. These were the end result and used in the launching of a near-space balloon by the Near-Space Balloon team.

The Process

Along with one other team member, here was the process working towards our task:

  • Researched information on temperature and pressure sensors and similar existing programs
  • Began implementation of program that collected temperature and pressure data from the surrounding environment using the sensors
  • Connected and soldered all the hardware components onto the microcontroller to begin testing of the program
  • Extensive testing of the program by doing test cases and changing surrounding environments
  • Merged our program into the main team code base and tested to ensure integration was seamless

Reflection

This was my first project as an intern and working on a team. I learned a new programming language and got a taste of working with hardware components, which is different to me because I mainly am comfortable with software. However, this experience also came with challenges. Our team did not receive our necessary equipment until the middle of our project, so we had to catch up more quickly once the equipment arrived because the deadline was approaching. Additionally, we had to account for other teammates' contribution as some were slower than others and communication was difficult because one of the team members was unresponsive at times. I overcame these obstacles by helping out others when I completed my tasks and taking charge where things needed to be taken care of. Despite these challenges, in the end, it was a successful project because it was used in the launching of a near-space balloon and teams reported back saying that the data was collected successfully.
